Early Farm Life
Sam grew up on the farm with dirt under his nails. He worked the field himself, learning the exact right sound a watermelon should make when you knock on it so it ripens just in time to be enjoyed by the roadside customers.

The first of four children, Sam has always been a born leader. He’s also a born communicator. Sam was put in charge of the public store on the farm at 16 and he learned the names and orders of all his customers by heart.

He met his wife Karen running the store, they shared a love of the outdoors but also a dream of city life.
Finding Peace in the City
After marrying, the couple moved to Springfield, Illinois. Sam took the first sales job he could get as Karen stayed home to care for their first child, Tommy. After years of working for his father and family, Sam had an independent streak he couldn’t shake. He knew being an office salesmen wasn’t right for him, or for his family.

It was in 1992 when he and his family traveled back to the farm for the holidays when he was talking to his brother Eddy that he came up with the idea to open Green Grocer in Springfield.

He saw the promise of bringing the rural charm and togetherness to a larger city and a larger community.
